THE PENINSULA HOTELS
In-Room Screens UI/UX Refresh
Peninsula was going to upgrade their in-room screen hardware and to improve the screen UI/UX at the same time. We supported their research & development team on the UI/UX refresh.
Role:
UX / UI Designer
Services:
Wireframe, Template Analysis, Grid Design, Cross-Device Design, Prototyping
Platform:
Android Tablet & Phone
Tools:
Sketch, Invision, JIRA
View fullsize
Where the story began
In a standard peninsula room, guests are offered to user 4 different screen devices in different sizes to control the room experience. The challenges of this project was to align different size of screen at the same time while keeping the consistent layout, look & feel at the same time. Changing one thing  could possibly changing the same thing on the other 3 devices. On top of that, the operation team are very picky on every changes as the change of screens affects all rooms.
Approach
We started this project by analysing the existing visual language and layout across all devices to make sure me and the UI designer understand how the icon, template, function, layout, visual are shared across different devices.
After we had a basic understanding, we started visiting the demonstration room to understand how users are navigating the screens in the actual environment and mapping out all the locations where the screen sits. By doing so, we had a further understanding of the context and ambient of the room of how those screens work from the user perspective.
The next thing was to conduct the gap analysis between new and old screens size to work on the information architecture of each of the screens. After all these, we then started to the production of the new screens.

Design Research Study
The Future of Luxury Hotels : How to survive in COVID19 Crisis and Beyond
As hotel industry is emerge from the recent economic downturn due to the pandemic, it is vital to scan ahead and understand what are the challenges and emerging opportunities that could be the innovative approach to the agenda for the hoteliers, especially luxury hotel sector.
Role:
Service Designer
Services:
UX Research, Qualitative Interview, Customer Journey Mapping, Data Analysis
View fullsize
Synthesis Wall - Themes
Design Challenge
In 2020, the travel and hospitality industry received a big hit due to the unprecedented pandemic - COVID-19. It is not uncommon to see news around hotel shut down or extremely low occupancy rate.
This triggers my interest in the future of the hotel industry in the post-COVID19 era. When this paper is started, the hotel industry seems started to accept the new normal Hence, this paper is to study the future challenges and opportunities of the hotel industry.
Using strategic design thinking and empirical users research, this paper hopes to give the industry practitioner mainly but limited to luxury hotels, some real world insights and opportunities from the customer point of view to cope with the future business challenges.
Methodology & Approach
This paper is following the core principle of convergence and divergence (which is known as double diamond) to explore the opportunity lies ahead. A modification is made to carter the limit of this paper, the processes are Discover, Analyze and Create.
Discover phase
, primary user research and secondary market research are conducted to understand what are the current and future challenges. The research target focuses on Millennials as they are the main economic drivers in the future.
Analyze phase
, from understanding to distil the insight from the research so as to define different themes into different personas.
Create phase
, by marrying the insight and personas to create some concept and overall strategic roadmap to overcome the challenges.

Different opportunities present different mix of options
Depending on the battle the luxury hotels would like to choose, hotels can design their services and direction in a strategic way via different combinations.
Customer Caretaker
To respond the COVID19, luxury hotels can have a better brand communication and trust to build up existing customer base and brand stickyness in midst and post COVID-19 crisis period.
Community Curator
To respond to the upcoming trend of biz-leisure travel pattern, luxury hotels need to reallocate their resources swiftly and precisely to capture the revenue.
Experiential Luxury
To respond to the increasing service expectation, luxury hotels can personalize their service to make their guest feel special.
In short
After making the strategic choices based on its own situation, it is where the real challenges start.
The strategic choice is more of a overall direction of how can the luxury hotels respond to the challenges. The path to excel the personalization services lies at how the data is being collected and inform the next decision.
A evidence-based experience design will plot out a clearheaded approach to develop a better customer experience and a truly personal experience to every customer.
